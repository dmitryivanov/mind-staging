Theme Usage & Style Guide

About Theme Meta

Could it get any more meta than this? It's the RailsThemes.com design, for your duplicitous pleasure!

When we decided to do a responsive redesign of RailsThemes.com, we thought: Hey, let's show 'em how it's done. Let's show 'em what a theme can do!

So here it is, Theme Meta!

The base theme is a delightful blue/green scheme with a responsive grid.

Special Features

  • Responsive design
  • Color changing (via. sass)
  • Matching jQuery UI theme (using the same color-changing ready sass)

Theme Layouts

This theme has the standard application and landing page styles. In addition, the standard application page accomodates pages with or without subnavigation.

Grid System

This theme is based on the Skeleton grid system.

You can view full documentation of the grid system at http://www.getskeleton.com/.

Gem Support

In addition to all of the standard styling/markup support, this gem includes special stylesheets for:

  • devise
  • formtastic
  • kaminari
  • simple_form
  • will_paginate
  • jquery_ui

Stylesheets are automatically included by the installer based on the contents of your Gemfile.

Getting Started

0. Check out the existing improvments to your app

All of your standard page elements (headers, tables, forms, etc.) should look pretty awesome already!

1. Check out the sample views

We include a variety of sample views to help you get started.

You can view them at:

  • http://localhost:3000/railsthemes/landing
  • http://localhost:3000/railsthemes/inner
  • http://localhost:3000/railsthemes/jquery_ui

2. Swap in your logo and app name

In the layouts/_header partial, swap in your application name and contact information.

In the layouts/_footer partial, swap in your application name (or logo) and update the navigation to match your app.

In the included layouts (landing and application) you may want to edit the title tag to include your application name.

3. Take advantage of theme-specific features

If you set content_for :page_title, "My Page Title in any your views, the page title will automatically be reflected on the page.

If you set content_for :title, "My Title in any your views, the page title will automatically be included in the title tag of the page.

Your theme also includes specific partials and grid options that you might want to try out. You can view demos of them in the sample views and in the official theme demo applications.

About RailsThemes

All of our themes take advantage of the Rails 3.1+ asset pipeline. As such, you'll find our generated files in the following locations:

Directory Structure

  • app
    • assets
      • images
      • javascripts
      • stylesheets
    • controllers
      • railsthemes (example only)
    • views
      • layouts
        • application
        • landing
        • _flash
        • _footer
        • _header
      • railsthemes
        • inner (example of general layout)
        • landing (example of landing layout)
        • jquery_ui (example of all jquery UI elements)
  • public
    • favicon.ico

Naming Conventions

Most theme files are either namespaced (e.g. railsthemes_themename_filename.ext) or in a railsthemes specific folder (e.g. railsthemes_themename/)

The exceptions to this are images and standard css/js assets from elsewhere (e.g. html5shiv).

Rails Version Support

Our themes are designed for Rails 3.1+
